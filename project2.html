Problem:

To increase user engagement and enhance the company's value proposition, we aim to provide a high-quality healthcare information subscription service that covers a wide range of health topics, including women's health and mindfulness.

Values for Company:

  • Increased user engagement: The personalized video recommendations and additional discovery points drive user engagement and time spent on the app, which could lead to higher user satisfaction and retention.
  • Higher adoption of primary services: Cross-service recommendations within video content can lead to increased adoption of other services like consultations and lab tests, driving additional revenue.

Values for Users:

  • Personalized content: Users will receive personalized video recommendations based on their interests, consultation history, or lab results.
  • Corporate benefit program access: Employees enrolled in corporate benefit programs can access specific topic or content package plans, increasing the value of their company-sponsored benefits.

Content Construct

  • There will be different categories and each category will have the video Topics (around 7 Topics to begin with). Each Topic will have 40-45 videos of 1 to 3 minutes duration for easy consumption.
  • Topics are: Women health (pregnancy preparation , lactation, etc), Yoga and Strength conditioning, and Mindfulness.

Subscription Model

A Freemium tiered Content Package model is a pricing model in which users are offered a basic level of content for free, and then charged a subscription fee for access to additional content, features, or capabilities. In the context of healthcare streaming platforms, a Freemium tiered Content Package model could work as follows

  • Free tier: Users can watch a limited number of videos for free, typically 2-3 videos per topic or category.
  • Premium tier: Users can subscribe to a premium tier for a monthly or annual fee. This will give them access to all of the videos in the library, as well as additional features and capabilities, such as the ability to download videos, create playlists, and leave comments.
  • The subscription price for the premium tier could vary depending on the topic or category. For example, a subscription for women's health content might be more expensive than a subscription for mindfulness content.
  • The Freemium tiered Content Package model is a popular choice for healthcare streaming platforms because it allows users to try out the platform before committing to a subscription. It also allows platforms to generate revenue from both free and paid users.

Internal Dashboard:

Internal staff use this dashboard to create video content and manage subscriptions. This includes:

  • Creating new video content: Internal staff can use the dashboard to upload and manage video files, create and edit video playlists, and add subtitles and captions to videos.
  • Managing subscriptions: Internal staff can use the dashboard to create and manage subscription plans, add and remove subscribers, and track subscription metrics such as churn rate and customer lifetime value.
  • Tracking and analyzing video engagement: Internal staff can use the dashboard to track video views, watch time, completion rate, and other engagement metrics. This data can be used to identify popular content, optimize video SEO, and improve the user experience.
  • Generating reports: Internal staff can use the dashboard to generate reports on video engagement, subscription metrics, and other key performance indicators (KPIs). This data can be used to track progress over time, identify areas for improvement, and make informed decisions about the video streaming platform.
