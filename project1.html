Online Consultation with Doctor

  •   Project Name

    Online Consultation with Doctor: Instant & Scheduled

  •   Contribution:

    Individual

  •   Timeline:

    June - August 2023

  •   Deliverable:

    Mobile App and Website

  •   Client Name:

    Confedential

Problem:

To provide an improved user experience for a diverse audience ranging from 18 to 60 years old, we must improve the current consultation design. Addressing the difficulties encountered by users of existing applications and creating an app with a user-friendly interface are essential for ensuring optimal usability.

Understand the patient pain points:

In this phase, I undertook user research to comprehend the pain points users encounter in other medical apps and to identify their expectations from a medical application. Additionally, I conducted market research, analyzing the features of existing medical apps to discern the most popular features and identify any missing functionalities.

  • The number of doctor consultations crosses 4 billion in India in FY21, spending a total of $26 billion on outpatient prescriptions. “It is likely to grow at 13 percent over the next three years,” said the report.
  • Before the pandemic, 3.5 million households were ordering medicines online, that number increased to 8.8 million after three months of the lockdown. (Report, the Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce).
  • Analysis indicates that 1 in 35 consultations will happen online by FY24. Practo, 1Mg, Pharmeasy, Mfine, Apollo, Netmeds, and a few apps are mostly used in India.

User Flow:

I first sketched out my ideas on paper. Then, I created a user flow and made dozens of corrections until I came up with the final one.

Data:

During the beta phase, we tracked user experience with this feature, including usability, functionality, and overall satisfaction. We analyzed the data collected during this phase using a combination of quantitative metrics, such as task completion rates and time on task, as well as qualitative feedback.

The results of the testing phase indicated that most participants found the app to be easy to use and effective in scheduling a consultation with a doctor. Participants reported a high level of satisfaction with the app, with a satisfaction rate of 90%. However, we found some insights from the beta version that will help us improve the feature.

My Moodboard:

I believe that one of the best ways to start designing a great UI is to consume a lot of great designs. I had all of the wireframes ready, but the UI needs to be carefully crafted so that it is accessible to all users. I have not only looked at these screenshots, but I have also checked out every app in the same category and tried to understand how they make their apps easy to use.

Visual Design

These screens create the first impression of the overall app. They are the best place to showcase the app's data and how it can help users. Therefore, I am trying to highlight the main screens of the feature that can make users want to get started with Medic.

Consultation Home Screen

After many iterations, I have finally created a design for the consultation screen. I understand that this screen is important because it is the main interface for users to access consultation features and functions. Therefore, I have designed it with a minimalist approach and incorporated the findings from my research to make it more user-friendly and accessible.

Video Consultation

The images show a chat conversation between a patient and a doctor. This chat platform is designed to provide patients with instant access to medical advice and support. It also allows patients to have a face-to-face consultation with their doctor without having to physically visit the clinic. Existing users can upload past medical reports to the platform and share them directly with their doctor during the consultation.

My key takeaways:

As a Product designer working on a Online Consultation Feature, I have gained valuable insights and knowledge throughout the design process. Some of the key things I have learned include

  • Understanding user needs was the starting point of the design process. I conducted research to understand the needs and pain points of users, including both patients and doctors. This helped me to identify the core features and functionalities that needed to be incorporated into the app.
  • The importance of simplicity was a key takeaway from the app design process. I learned that it is important to keep the user interface simple and intuitive. A complex and cluttered design can be confusing and frustrating for users, especially older individuals who may not be tech-savvy.
  • Incorporating user feedback was an essential part of the design process. I received regular feedback from users and made adjustments to the feature accordingly. This helped me to ensure that the feature was meeting their needs and expectations, and to make any necessary changes to improve the overall user experience.

In conclusion, designing an online consultation feature has been a challenging and rewarding experience. It has allowed me to gain a deeper understanding of the needs of users and develop my skills as a product designer.
